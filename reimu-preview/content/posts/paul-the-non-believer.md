Paul explicitly wrote in letters like 1 Thessalonians that he expected the world to end and Jesus to return during his own lifetime. Because he was a first-century apocalyptic Jew expecting immediate, physical intervention from God, a 2,000-year delay means his timeline failed, and he would view later theological explanations for this delay as pure copium. Finally, while Jesus preached a Jewish message to other Jews, Paul was the architect of mainstream Christianity; he is the one who turned a small Jewish sect into a distinct religion by establishing the core doctrines of salvation through faith and expanding the movement to the non-Jewish world.
